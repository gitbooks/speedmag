FAQ

Got questions?

Which banks are supported?

SpeedMag can import PDF statements from Navy Federal Credit Union and CSV exports from Chase, Bank of America, Capital One, Wells Fargo, and Citi. It also supports a generic CSV format, so virtually any bank that lets you download transactions as CSV will work.

Is my financial data safe?

Absolutely. SpeedMag stores everything locally on your Mac — in your standard Application Support folder. Your data is never uploaded anywhere. There are no accounts, no logins, and no servers. If it's on your Mac, it's yours alone.

What reports can I generate?

SpeedMag includes five report types: Profit & Loss statements, Balance Sheets, Cash Flow reports, Expense Breakdowns (with pie charts), and Tax Summaries. All reports can be filtered by year and exported to PDF. Every feature is included in the $5/month plan.

What's included in the $5/month?

Everything. There are no tiers, no feature gates, and no per-user fees. You get every feature, every update, unlimited users, and unlimited businesses. New bank formats, new reports, and new features are delivered automatically at no extra cost. Cancel anytime.

Can I track inventory?

Yes! SpeedMag includes full inventory management — add items with SKUs, track stock levels, set reorder points, and record purchases, sales, and adjustments. Cost of Goods Sold (COGS) automatically flows into your P&L and inventory value appears on your Balance Sheet as an asset.

Does it work on Windows or Linux?

SpeedMag is currently a native macOS app (macOS 12+). Windows and Linux versions are on the roadmap — join our mailing list to be notified when they're available.

Why is SpeedMag so affordable?

Because SpeedMag runs locally on your Mac — no expensive cloud infrastructure, no per-user server costs. We pass those savings directly to you. $5/month for a full bookkeeping platform is possible when you're not paying for someone else's servers.
